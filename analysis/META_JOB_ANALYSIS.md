## ATS Strategy (Meta uses Greenhouse)
- Submit as .docx for best parsing
- Simple single-column layout, no tables, no graphics, no headers/footers
- Standard section headings: "Professional Summary", "Experience", "Education", "Skills"
- Mirror exact keywords from JD (e.g., "electro-mechanical", "root cause analysis", "DFM/DFA", "cross-functional")
- Spell out acronyms AND include abbreviation: "Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T)"
- Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri)

## Anti-AI-Detection Strategy
- Use specific, verifiable details (company names, product lines, sensor models)
- Vary sentence length and structure naturally
- Include domain jargon only someone in the field would know (MTBF, MTTR, Savitzky-Golay)
- Reference real product names and part numbers (UFactory 850, FUTEK USB520)
- Mix quantified and qualitative bullets
- Avoid buzzword stacking and generic filler phrases
- Write in a voice that reflects your actual experience level, not a template

## Meta Culture Keywords to Weave In
- "Move fast" — show you ship quickly and iterate
- "Focus on impact" — quantify outcomes
- "Be bold" — show initiative (you were hired to bring automation to a manual facility)
- "Cross-functional collaboration" — manufacturing, quality, engineering teams
- "Ambiguous environments" — evolving hardware, new processes
